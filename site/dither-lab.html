dither lab

dither-kit (tripwire.sh) ported to vanilla ES modules in js/visuals/dither.js — no React, no Tailwind, no npm. Everything below runs on the real data.json. Left panel = what the site does today, right = the dithered version.

1 · the heartbeat — timeline area fill

Chart.js keeps the axes, tooltip, animation and the peak label. Only the area fill changes: the dataset's backgroundColor gradient is switched off and ditherFillPlugin blits the Bayer texture over the plot rect.

← hover the chart itself: the dither eases denser and the stars brighten
now — Chart.js linear gradient
dithered — ditherFillPlugin (hover it)

2 · the leaderboard — bar fills

Same bars, same per-person colours. The dithered side drops ditherGradient (direction right, hue from each boi's hex) into the fill div.

← hover a row: the bar lifts too
now — CSS linear-gradient
dithered — ditherGradient

3 · the bois — generative avatars

Seeded from the display name via FNV-1a → xorshift32, so a boi's glyph never changes. Hue is drawn from the same stream, or overridden with his site colour.

4 · buttons

Real <button> elements with the canvas behind the label. Hover eases the dither denser, pressing lifts it further.

0 · the standalone renderer — no Chart.js

`ditherChart()` from dither-chart.js owns the whole render path: scales, grid, axes, the dither surface, the scrub crosshair, tooltip and legend. Zero dependencies. Multi-series (top 4 bois by month), so stacking and the legend spotlight have something to do — hover a legend entry to spotlight it, click to lock.

ditherChart — its own engine

0b · polar + sparklines — the rest of the engine

ditherPie / ditherRadar from dither-polar.js — the disc dithers dense at the rim and thins to the centre; the radar polygons dither dense at the edge (distance-to-edge drives density, upstream's rule). Same legend spotlight, tooltip and entrance as the cartesian engine. ditherSparkline is the engine shrunk to "values in a box". Hover slices, spokes and legends.

pie — shares by boi (top 6 + the rest)
radar — top 3 bois, each axis normalised to its own max
sparklines — monthly shares, top 4 (hover one)

5 · gradient washes

Static one-shot paints — cheap enough for section fades, card backdrops, the outro footer.
